Evergreen trees are fascinating because they stay green all year, even in harsh weather. Their needle-shaped or waxy leaves help them save water, making them perfect for cold or dry regions. These trees not only provide oxygen but also act as natural windbreakers, protecting the environment. Did you know? Evergreen trees like pines and spruces are also used as Christmas trees, bringing joy to millions every year!

Deciduous Trees

Deciduous trees are unique because they shed their leaves every autumn, preparing for the colder months ahead. This process helps the trees conserve water and energy during winter when resources are scarce. In spring, they grow fresh, green leaves that help them make food through photosynthesis. Trees like oak, maple, and birch belong to this category and are known for their stunning fall colors, ranging from bright yellow to deep red. These trees not only enhance the beauty of landscapes but also provide habitats for various birds and animals.

The vibrant colors of deciduous trees in autumn come from pigments already present in the leaves. When chlorophyll (the green pigment) breaks down, the hidden yellows, oranges, and reds are revealed, creating the magical fall scenery we love!

Mangrove Trees

Mangrove trees are remarkable plants that thrive in coastal areas where freshwater meets the salty sea. They have unique roots that grow above the water, allowing them to breathe and stay stable in muddy conditions. These trees play a crucial role in protecting shorelines from erosion and storms while acting as a natural filter for pollutants. Mangroves are also home to a diverse range of wildlife, including fish, crabs, and migratory birds. Beyond their environmental importance, they provide livelihoods for local communities through fishing and sustainable wood collection.
